Musical Features Modification for Less Intrusive Delivery of Popular Notification Sounds

We present a novel method to deliver popular artificial notification sounds in a less intrusive but still adequately noticeable manner. We collected 81 commonly used notification sounds from iOS/Android systems and popular applications like Skype, please refer to our notification sound dataset.

Since digital music has been widespread and many people have the habit of conducting activities over music, we develop our notification delivery method by using the concept of "embedding notification into the user's music". More specifically, we first transfer the musical timbre of a notification sound into the timbre of the background music that a user is listening to, while preserving the original notification melody envelope to still associate the notification with its interpretation. Next, we harmoniously embed the timbre-transformed notification into the background music for delivery. In order to achieve a seamless integration, we adjust the amplitude, tempo, and the overall octave level of the notification to match these features of the music, and we embed the notification into music with fade-in and fade-out effects, while the embedding spot is selected by algorithmically checking and finding the segment of the music that shares the most similar musical features with the notification sound.



Part I: Notification Timbre Transfer

We implement a melody extraction-based approach to notification timbre transfer. To this end, we extend the original CREPE model to achieve a better melody extraction performance. After melody extraction, we implement timbre transfer using MIDI interface. In the following, we show examples of notification timbre transfer that are based on (1) the original CREPE model and (2) our extended model. Our model can better preserve the original notification melody.
